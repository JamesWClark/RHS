Mission Week Raises $43,000

Since its inception in 1970, Mission Week has been a fun and important week at Rockhurst High School.

Each grade level selected a national or international charitable organization to be the recipient of the class' fundraising.

Freshmen: St. Peter Claver Jesuit Parish in Belize
Sophomores: Intsikelelo in Cape Town, Africa
Juniors: Habitat for Humanity Appalachia
Seniors: Free the Children & Esperanza

During the week, which included dress down days, faculty/student basketball game, penny wars and the annual board game, Rockhurst students raised more than $43,000.

Seniors Thanked for Service 

Each year, the first 2.5 weeks of second semester begins with the senior class out of the building, and working in charity organizations, both locally and internationally.

Students are either placed with a local agency, or can participate in one of the immersion trips to Tijuana or Guatemala, which are in conjunction with the Total Ignatian Experience (TIE) trips.

Their great work is appreciated by the community.  One senior, Jack Ruhl, spent his senior service with the special needs program at Shawnee Mission East.  They put together a video from his time spent with those students.  You can watch it here.

Four Named Presidential Scholar Candidates

The U.S. Presidential Scholars Program was established in 1964, by executive order of the President, to recognize and honor some of our nation's most distinguished graduating high school seniors.

The program is designed to recognize some of our nation's most distinguished graduating seniors for their accomplishments in many areas: academic success, leadership, and service to school and community. Each year, up to 161 students are named as Presidential Scholars, one of the nation's highest honors for high school students.

The Rockhurst High School Class of 2016 celebrates four students who have been selected as candidates for this prestigious program. Congratulations to Daniel Henry, Michael Jenia, Blaise Rebman, and Colin Slaymaker.

Alums Part of Hampton University History

Jourdan Ellis '13 and Leo Jackson '15 are on the Hampton University Lacrosse team, which is in its inaugural year as a varsity program.  

Hampton's first game, which was on Saturday, Feb. 13, was the first NCAA Division I men's team from a historically black college or university in 35 years.

"The story behind our team speaks volumes," said Jourdan, a junior on academic scholarship and didn't intend to play lacrosse until being introduced to the game last year.  "This is a movie-type thing.  To be part of this fills my heart with so much joy."

Stinger '05 Awarded Young Engineer Honor

Stephen Stinger '05, a Civil/Structural Engineer at Black & Veatch was awarded "Outstanding Young Civil Engineer in the Private Sector" at the ASCE Central Region Younger Member Council 2016.

Stephen has been with Black & Veatch since July 2011 and has worked on the design of reinforced concrete foundations and structural steel superstructures.  He has also worked on multiple air quality control systems (AQCS), coal, nuclear and oil & gas projects.

Fitzpatrick '74 a "Missourian You Sould Know"

Mark Fitzpatrick, "The Voice of the Hawklets," as he is the PA announcer for Rockhurst football games, has been named to Ingram's Magazine's list of "50 Missourians You Should Know.  Ingram's wrote the following about Mark:

You might not know Mark Fitzpatrick, but if you’re into Big 12 basketball, you may have heard him: This committed sports nut, a banker by day, has served as PA announcer for the Big 12’s tournament at Sprint Center, and he was an official scorer for MU baseball in 1980, and he has worked the scoreboard at Kauffman Stadium. 

Omaha-born but raised in Kansas City from age 4, Fitzpatrick says “I had a real normal, happy childhood, became a big sports fan at a very young age and tried to play all of the sports—though none of them very well.” 

He’s a Rockhurst High and Mizzou journalism graduate who worked in sports information for three years, first at Stanford University, then back in Columbia, where he decided to get an MBA. “Mark Twain Bank made me the best offer, so I became a banker,” he says. The best part about that? “Working with so many different businesses,” says Fitzpatrick, who oversees commercial loans for Missouri Bank & Trust. “I have had the opportunity to work with people in a wide variety of industries and it’s been great to share in their successes.”

Alums Open Cryotherapy Salon


John Sirna '92 and Frank Tarantino '93 have opened CryoSalon in Overland Park, KS.  CryoSalon is a recovery center offering Whole Body Cryotherapy and NormaTec Recovery.

Whole Body Cryotherapy is a hyper-cooling process that provides your body relief of inflammation, pain and soreness by surrounding your body with extremely cold air temperatures averaging -240 degrees Fahrenheit in a Cryo booth for up to three minutes.

Routine Whole Body Cryotherapy sessions have been shown to increase your athletic recovery & performance, the wellness of your body, the management of your pain, and keep you looking young and beautiful.

Rockhurst Launches Mobile App

With the help of Tom DeBacco '77 and his team at Essenza Software, Rockhurst has launched a mobile application to help family, friends and alumni of Rockhurst stay updated on the happenings at The Rock.

Still in its early phase, the app is a platform that centralizes information posted to the school's website and social media outlets.  The calendar feature also allows you to personalize what events you may want to sync to your own calendar.

The app is available for download, and is called "Rockhurst High School News."  Any feedback about the app's performance or features is appreciated.
